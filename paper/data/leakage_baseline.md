# Source/Surface Leakage Baselines for the CoT Pattern Corpus

## Why this matters

Adversarial critics of an earlier hybrid corpus (mixing Denison reward-hacking
transcripts + FaithCoT-Bench post-hoc data + PRM800K math errors + synthetic
items) found a **fatal source-label confound**: each of the 5 patterns came
from a single dataset, so a discovery system could achieve near-perfect
purity by detecting source artifacts (bash tags, "Step N:" prefixes, file
paths, truncation lengths) without learning anything about the named
patterns.

We quantified this and pivoted.

## Leakage measurements

We trained three baselines on the text → pattern mapping:

- **TF-IDF + Logistic Regression** under 5-fold stratified CV — supervised.
- **14 surface features** (length, counts of `\boxed`, `Step`, `$`, `\frac`, newlines, `**`, `User`, `Wait`, `the grader`, `the answer is`, `Reconsideration`, `hack`, `reward`) + LR under 5-fold stratified CV — supervised.
- **K-means (k=5) on the same 14 surface features** with optimal cluster-to-class mapping via the Hungarian algorithm — **unsupervised** (median over 5 random seeds). This is the proper head-to-head with taxonomy_agent's discovery purity.

| Corpus | TF-IDF supervised | Surface supervised | **Surface unsupervised** | Source-only | Random | Verdict |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Hybrid (4 sources)** | 76% | **95%** | (not computed) | 89% | 20-25% | UNUSABLE — surface alone separates patterns |
| **Synthetic-only-math500** | 26% | 73% | **50%** | n/a (1 source) | 20% | ACCEPTABLE — proper unsupervised baseline well below taxonomy_agent |

The synthetic-only corpus has **dramatically less surface leakage** in
TF-IDF features (26% vs 76%). More importantly, the *unsupervised*
surface-feature baseline (k-means) achieves 50% purity — the proper
comparator for taxonomy_agent's unsupervised discovery. The supervised
ceiling at 73% is an oracle bound (it sees gold labels during training),
not a head-to-head baseline.

## Repairs applied

Both corpora had the following post-processing applied before measurement:

1. **Sycophantic items**: appended user cue to problem text — the construct
   requires a user belief visible in the problem, not just referenced in the
   CoT. The original generator embedded the user-belief in the prompt to the
   CoT-writer but didn't include it in the saved problem text; we patched
   this.
2. **Unfaithful paraphrase items**: stripped explicit "Paraphrase:" /
   "Revised Problem:" / "I will change X to Y" markers — these were
   generator tells, not part of the construct.
3. **Universal**: collapsed double "Reasoning: Reasoning:" prefixes.

## Decision: ship the synthetic-only-math500 corpus

**Corpus statistics:**
- 149 items
- 5 patterns × ~30 each (one at 29 from upstream verifier rejection)
- All sourced from `math500_l4_5` (level 4-5 problems from MATH-500)
- Generated by Gemini-3.1-Flash-Lite under pattern-specific injector prompts
- Verified by Claude Opus 4.8 majority-vote ensemble

**Limitations honestly disclosed in the paper:**

1. Single domain (competition math). Sycophancy and reward-hacking might
   manifest differently on non-math tasks, and we cannot test that here.
2. Synthetic generation. The CoTs are produced by Gemini under explicit
   pattern-targeting prompts — they are caricatures of the patterns rather
   than naturalistic occurrences in deployed model outputs.
3. **Three distinct baselines**: random (20%), unsupervised k-means on
   surface features (50%), and supervised oracle on surface features (73%).
   The unsupervised k-means is the proper apples-to-apples comparator —
   taxonomy_agent's purity meaningfully exceeding 50% indicates real
   discovery beyond what surface features alone afford. Exceeding the 73%
   supervised oracle is a stronger claim (the system finds structure that
   supervised baselines miss).
4. The 5 patterns are not exhaustive of the safety/alignment CoT-failure
   literature — we chose them for their 2024-2026 citation centrality and
   their elicit-ability via simple injector prompts.

## Why we didn't use real CoT data

We investigated 5 public datasets (Sharma sycophancy-eval, Perez evals,
FaithCoT-Bench, Denison sycophancy-to-subterfuge, PRM800K). Findings:

- Only **Denison** ships real CoTs annotated for the corresponding pattern
  (reward-hacking), and only ~7 explicit-tampering examples.
- **FaithCoT-Bench** has post-hoc labels but they are statistical proxies
  (step-removal AUC), not the structural "answer-first" construct we test.
- **PRM800K** has -1-rated steps but they conflate arithmetic slips with
  hallucinated premises and require a re-labeling judge pass.
- **Sharma** and **Perez** are stimuli (prompts designed to elicit
  sycophancy), not annotated CoT pivots.
- **Chen et al. 2025** measures CoT-pivot sycophancy directly but the data
  is unreleased.

Combining these into a multi-source corpus produced the source-label
confound shown above. We therefore prioritized methodological soundness
over ecological validity and report on a controlled synthetic corpus, with
the leakage baselines published as evaluative context.
