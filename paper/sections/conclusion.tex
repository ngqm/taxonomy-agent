\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We present an orchestrator/judge agent built on two structural commitments: a typed dispatcher with validate-before-mutate and per-operation failure isolation, and a stateless judge that re-derives each item's category from item text alone. Per-item cost is linear in the corpus and runs on the cheap model, and the orchestrator cannot force a label past the judge. At the install default (DeepSeek-v4-Flash for both roles), the system reaches NMI $0.727$ on 20~Newsgroups at USD $0.17$ per run, beating a model-matched embed-cluster-LLM reimplementation of TnT-LLM by $1.5\times$, and reaches NMI $0.944$ across three seeds on a 149-item synthetic CoT-failure corpus, labelling on average $142$ of $149$ items at $100\%$ accuracy. A Sonnet 4.6 orchestrator pair at eleven times the cost sits inside the cheap pair's NMI interval on 20NG, so orchestrator quality is not the bottleneck the field assumes.
