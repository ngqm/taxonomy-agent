\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

Taxonomy induction tools rarely survive the trip from research prototype to something a stranger can run at a booth. We present an open-source orchestrator--judge agent whose primary contribution is the operational layer that closes that gap: live native provider cost accounting split by orchestrator and judge, partial-progress streaming so an interrupted run leaves recoverable state, vendor-agnostic routing, and a browser UI for live inspection. Beneath sit a typed dispatcher that validates each edit before mutation and a parallel stateless judge. A single-seed ablation that swaps the typed dispatcher for a loose one stalls the orchestrator at zero revise calls (\S\ref{sec:eval-results-cot}), and the judge's don't-fit signal saturates at the floor and does not function as a quantitative leading indicator; we report both honestly. On 20~Newsgroups the system leads the one-shot LLM baseline by $1.9\times$ on NMI and $3.3\times$ on ARI, retains a $1.3\times$ NMI and $1.4\times$ ARI lead over an embed-then-LLM-label pipeline, and a $2.6\times$ ARI margin over tuned BERTopic; on a 149-item synthetic corpus of chain-of-thought failures it labels 109 of 149 items at $100\%$ accuracy and reaches $0.732$ coverage-adjusted purity at parity with a supervised surface oracle. Taxonomy induction is now operable from a demo booth alongside the results table.
