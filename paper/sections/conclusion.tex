\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We presented \textsc{taxonomy\_agent}, an open-source orchestrator--judge agent for iterative taxonomy discovery. Its contribution is a careful engineering recomposition: a typed revise DSL of six verbs, validated before mutation, constrains the orchestrator's edit surface; a parallel judge decouples labelling from discovery; and operational tooling (cost accounting, partial-progress streaming, a browser UI) closes the gap between a research prototype and a tool a stranger can use at a demo booth. On 20~Newsgroups (three independently sampled random seeds, $n{=}487$), the system leads the LLM baselines by $1.9\times$ on NMI and retains a $2.6\times$ ARI lead over aggressively-tuned BERTopic. On a 149-item synthetic benchmark of chain-of-thought failure patterns from the 2024--2026 alignment literature, it produces precise labels on the items it chooses, though the supervised metrics cannot cleanly separate semantic discovery from sharper surface extraction. A naturalistic CoT corpus with multiple sources per pattern would dissociate the two signals by construction, turning a demo-ready prototype into a load-bearing instrument for alignment research. Code, corpora, and reproduction scripts are at \url{https://github.com/ngqm/taxonomy-agent}.
