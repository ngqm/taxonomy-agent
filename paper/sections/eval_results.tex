\subsection{Evaluation Methodology}
\label{sec:eval-method}

We run \textsc{taxonomy\_agent} with a Claude~Sonnet~4.6 orchestrator
and a DeepSeek-v4-Flash judge on both benchmarks; the
\textsc{single\_shot} and \textsc{iterative\_proposal} baselines also
use DeepSeek-v4-Flash, BERTopic uses no LLM, and the installed system
default is a Llama-3.3-70B judge that we swap to DeepSeek-v4-Flash for
the eval runs.\footnote{The installed default judge is
Llama-3.3-70B-Instruct; we override to DeepSeek-v4-Flash on the
benchmarks for a comparable-quality but cheaper publication pair.}
For 20~Newsgroups we report mean $\pm$ standard deviation over three
independently sampled random seeds on a 487-document stratified
subsample (sampled at 25 items per gold class; 13 documents are
dropped after sklearn's default header/footer/quote stripping). All
four methods see the same documents under the same seed. BERTopic
appears at its library default \texttt{min\_topic\_size=10} in the
main table; a sweep over $\{2,5,10,15,25\}$ is summarised below and
detailed in Appendix~A.

\paragraph{Supervised metrics.}
Taxonomy\_agent leads the LLM and clustering baselines on the metrics
that resist splitting: ARI 0.336 against single\_shot's 0.101,
iterative\_proposal's 0.043, and embed\_cluster\_llm's 0.243 (a
$1.4\times$ lead over the strongest LLM-driven comparator), and NMI
0.656 against 0.345 and 0.488 (a $1.9\times$ and $1.3\times$ lead).
Cross-seed standard deviations are smaller for taxonomy\_agent than
for the LLM baselines (NMI std 0.012 against single\_shot's 0.056),
which is consistent with the agentic loop converging to a similar
mid-granularity structure on each run (7--8 categories across the
three seeds). On purity the system leads the LLM baselines at 0.367
against single\_shot's 0.253 but is beaten by embed\_cluster\_llm
($K{=}16$) and by a tuned BERTopic that splits the corpus into roughly
60 micro-clusters; both win purity by emitting much finer-grained
categories, a granularity confound covered next.

\paragraph{Intrinsic coherence and tuned BERTopic.}
Coherence is not uniformly in taxonomy\_agent's favour:
iterative\_proposal wins NPMI by emitting 16 lexically focused
categories, and LDA and embed\_cluster\_llm hold a small $C_v$ lead by
keeping $K{=}16$ fixed. BERTopic at library defaults wins $C_v$ at
0.767 but with a degenerate two-cluster solution that loses every
supervised metric. Under a sweep of \texttt{min\_topic\_size}
(Appendix~A), the most aggressive setting emits roughly 60
micro-clusters and closes the purity gap, but ARI then sits at
$0.127\pm.038$ against our $0.336\pm.035$, a $2.6\times$ lead: ARI
penalises the over-splitting that boosts BERTopic's purity, so the lead
is robust on the metric that balances lumping against splitting.

\paragraph{Cost.}
A taxonomy\_agent run on 20NG costs USD\,1.86 on average; the
orchestrator contributes 96--98\%\ of total spend because LangChain's
\texttt{bind\_tools} path strips the Anthropic prompt-caching markers
from OpenRouter requests, an implementation bug discussed in
\S\ref{sec:limitations}. Wall-clock time of 258\,s is roughly
$1.5\times$ the LLM baselines and $17\times$ BERTopic; absolute spend
remains under USD\,2 for the 487-document corpus, well below the
USD\,0.10--USD\,1.00 per item rates typical of human codebook
construction.

\paragraph{Pattern recovery.}
Taxonomy\_agent labels 109 of 149 items and every prediction matches
the gold pattern ($109/109 = 100\%$ accuracy on labelled items); the
remaining 40 are routed to \texttt{other}. Coverage-adjusted purity
treating \texttt{other} as a non-match is $109/149 = 0.732$, at
parity with a 0.730 supervised surface-feature oracle that has
gold-label access (logistic regression on 14 surface features, 5-fold
stratified CV) and $+23$ points above an unsupervised k-means baseline
at $k=5$ on the same features (Appendix~A details the calibration
baselines and the commensurability caveat). All LLM and clustering
discovery baselines stay below NMI 0.15, including embed\_cluster\_llm
at 0.039 (essentially random), so iterative refinement is doing
real work on this task. Precision-at-coverage holds at $100\%$ across
the four mathematical sub-domains the corpus spans (Appendix~A,
Table~A1).

\paragraph{Ablation.}
A single-seed loose-dispatcher ablation that strips the typed revise
dispatcher's source-existence and name-collision checks stalled at
zero revise calls in 90\,s before we terminated it; the typed run on
the same configuration finishes at 197\,s with one successful
\textsc{revise} call. A single seed cannot isolate the dispatcher
from ordinary stochasticity; we report this honestly as preliminary
support and develop the two consistent interpretations in
Appendix~A and \S\ref{sec:limitations}.

\paragraph{Failure mode.}
Across both corpora, the system's consistent failure mode is
\emph{lumping}: on 20NG it converges on coherent 7--8-category
macro-clusters that fuse semantically related fine-grained gold
classes (e.g., the three \texttt{talk.politics.*} gold classes
collapse to one ``politics and society'' category), and on CoT the
\texttt{hallucinated\_premise} pattern, which lacks the strong lexical
tells the other four exhibit, recovers only 9 of roughly 30 gold
instances. The differential under-recovery is evidence consistent
with sharper surface extraction rather than additional semantic
content, the interpretive caveat carried in \S\ref{sec:limitations}.
The on-disk \texttt{trace.jsonl} surfaces every tool call, op, and
classify probe so an analyst can audit the run; a worked example and
the Streamlit Inspect timeline are in Appendix~A.
