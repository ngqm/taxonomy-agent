\paragraph{Run configuration.}
We run \textsc{taxonomy\_agent} at the install default
(DeepSeek-v4-Flash for both orchestrator and judge) on both
benchmarks; the \textsc{single\_shot} and \textsc{iterative\_proposal}
baselines also use DeepSeek-v4-Flash, and BERTopic uses no LLM. A
Sonnet 4.6 orchestrator pair is reported as the optional quality

appears at its library default \texttt{min\_topic\_size=10} in the

\paragraph{Supervised metrics.}
At the install default, \textsc{taxonomy\_agent} leads every LLM and
clustering baseline on the three supervised metrics: purity $0.569$
against embed\_cluster\_llm's $0.434$, NMI $0.740$ against $0.488$
(a $1.5\times$ lead over the strongest LLM-driven comparator), and
ARI $0.489$ against $0.243$ (a $2.0\times$ lead). The lead over
single\_shot is $2.1\times$ on NMI and $4.8\times$ on ARI; the
\textsc{iterative\_proposal} TopicGPT port loses to single\_shot on
every supervised metric, which appears to come from refinement drift
on a cheap proposer.%
\footnote{Cross-seed stddev on the cheap pair (NMI $0.055$) is
larger than the Sonnet pair ($0.012$); the cheap run varies between
$8$ and $16$ categories, the Sonnet run lumps to $7$--$8$. One
seed entered a monotonic-add loop on first attempt
(\S\ref{sec:limitations}); a re-run with a different
non-deterministic trajectory converged, and we added an
auto-finalize fallback that labels the corpus against the on-disk
taxonomy when the orchestrator ends without an artifact.}
The Sonnet orchestrator row is included for cost comparison and
sits inside the cheap pair's NMI interval ($0.656 \pm 0.012$ vs
$0.740 \pm 0.055$) at roughly eleven times the cost. We therefore
make the cheap pair the install and publication default. The
concurrent \citet{brady2025iterativetax} pipeline at default
produces a degenerate $3$-cluster solution
(NMI $0.164 \pm 0.092$, $58\%$ HDBSCAN noise), shape-matched to
BERTopic at defaults; a \texttt{min\_cluster\_size} sweep does not
lift the mean.

\paragraph{Intrinsic coherence and tuned BERTopic.}
Coherence is not uniformly in taxonomy\_agent's favour:
iterative\_proposal wins NPMI by emitting 16 lexically focused
categories, and LDA and embed\_cluster\_llm hold a small $C_v$ lead by
keeping $K{=}16$ fixed. BERTopic at library defaults wins $C_v$ at
$0.767$ but with a degenerate two-cluster solution that loses every
supervised metric. Under a sweep of \texttt{min\_topic\_size}
(Appendix~A), the most aggressive setting emits roughly 60
micro-clusters and closes the purity gap, but ARI then sits at
$0.127 \pm .038$ against our $0.452 \pm .096$, a $3.6\times$ lead.

\paragraph{Cost.}
A cheap-pair \textsc{taxonomy\_agent} run on 20NG costs USD\,$0.16$
on average, about $7\times$ the cost of embed\_cluster\_llm at the
same labeller and roughly $1/12$ of the Sonnet orchestrator pair.
Wall-clock time of $548\,$s is $3\times$ the LLM baselines and
$37\times$ BERTopic; absolute spend remains below USD\,$0.20$ on the
487-document corpus, well below the USD\,$0.10$--USD\,$1.00$ per item
rates typical of human codebook construction. On the Sonnet pair the
orchestrator contributes 96--98\%\ of total spend because LangChain's
\texttt{bind\_tools} path strips Anthropic prompt-caching markers from
OpenRouter requests; the cheap pair is unaffected because DeepSeek
provider-side caching does not depend on the markers
(\S\ref{sec:limitations}).

\paragraph{Pattern recovery.}
At the install default and averaged across three seeds,
\textsc{taxonomy\_agent} labels roughly $142$ of $149$ items per run
($95\%$ coverage) and every prediction matches the gold pattern.
Coverage-adjusted purity averages $0.95$, $+22$ points above a
$0.730$ supervised surface-feature oracle that has gold-label
access (logistic regression on 14 surface features, 5-fold
stratified CV). Per-pattern recovery is uniform across seeds:
sycophantic capitulation $83/90$, unfaithful paraphrase $89/90$,
post-hoc rationalization $86/90$, hallucinated premise $81/87$,
reward-hack verbalization $88/90$. The Sonnet orchestrator pair
recovers only $109$ of $149$ items ($0.732$ coverage-adjusted
purity) and under-recovers \texttt{hallucinated\_premise} at
$9/29$; the cheap pair closes that gap at roughly one-thirtieth the
cost. All LLM and clustering discovery baselines stay below NMI
$0.15$, including embed\_cluster\_llm at $0.039$ (essentially
random), so iterative refinement is doing real work on this task.

\paragraph{Ablation.}
A single-seed loose-dispatcher ablation that strips the typed revise
dispatcher's source-existence and name-collision checks stalled at
zero revise calls in 90\,s before we terminated it; the typed run on
the same configuration finishes at 197\,s with one successful
\textsc{revise} call. A single seed cannot isolate the dispatcher
from ordinary stochasticity; we report this honestly as preliminary
support and develop the two consistent interpretations in
Appendix~A and \S\ref{sec:limitations}.

\paragraph{Failure mode.}
The Sonnet pair lumps semantically related gold classes
(\texttt{talk.politics.*} collapses to one macro-cluster, and CoT
\texttt{hallucinated\_premise} recovers at $9/29$). The cheap pair
keeps finer distinctions but is occasionally tripped by a
monotonic-add loop: seed 46 emitted $8$ revise calls all
\textsc{add}, growing the taxonomy from $10$ to $21$ categories
without converging. \texttt{trace.jsonl} surfaces the failure
(no \textsc{merge}/\textsc{drop}/\textsc{edit}) within seconds; we
added an auto-finalize fallback that labels the corpus against the
on-disk taxonomy and a re-run converged.
