\paragraph{\textsc{TaxonomyAgent} leads supervised metrics.}
\textsc{TaxonomyAgent} leads every LLM and
clustering baseline on the three supervised metrics: purity $0.569$
against \texttt{topicgpt}'s $0.542$ (the strongest LLM comparator on

comparator), and ARI $0.489$ against $0.297$ (a $1.65\times$ lead).
The lead over single\_shot is $2.1\times$ on NMI and $4.8\times$ on
ARI; \texttt{iterative\_proposal} loses to single\_shot on every
supervised metric, suggesting that iterative label refinement
without a separate judge does not help on this corpus.%

categories across the five seeds. One seed entered a monotonic-add
loop on first attempt (\S\ref{sec:limitations}) and was re-sampled at
a different sampling temperature; we added an auto-finalize fallback
that labels the corpus against the on-disk taxonomy when the
orchestrator ends without an artifact, and the re-sampled seed
finished without invoking it. The fallback was applied uniformly to
all five seeds reported in Table~\ref{tab:20ng}.}
The concurrent \citet{brady2025iterativetax} pipeline at default
produces a degenerate $3$-cluster solution
(NMI $0.164 \pm 0.092$, $58\%$ HDBSCAN noise), with the same
small-cluster, high-noise shape as BERTopic at library defaults; a
\texttt{min\_cluster\_size} sweep does not raise mean NMI.

\paragraph{Coherence trades off against supervised quality.}
Coherence is not uniformly in \textsc{TaxonomyAgent}'s favour:
iterative\_proposal wins NPMI by emitting 16 lexically focused
categories, and LDA and embed\_cluster\_llm lead $C_v$ by about
$0.04$ by keeping $K{=}16$ fixed. BERTopic at library defaults wins $C_v$ at
$0.767$ but with a degenerate two-cluster solution that loses every
supervised metric. Under a sweep of \texttt{min\_topic\_size}
in the released data, the most aggressive setting emits roughly 60
micro-clusters and closes the purity gap, but ARI then sits at
$0.127 \pm .038$ against our $0.489 \pm .085$, a $3.85\times$ lead.

\paragraph{Absolute spend stays under \$0.20 per 20NG run.}
A \textsc{TaxonomyAgent} run on 20NG costs $\$0.17$ on average, about
$8\times$ the cost of embed\_cluster\_llm at the same labeller.
Wall-clock time of $713\,$s is $4\times$ the LLM baselines and
$48\times$ BERTopic; absolute spend remains below $\$0.20$ on the
487-document corpus.

\paragraph{\textsc{TaxonomyAgent} leads every baseline on DarkBench.}
\textsc{TaxonomyAgent} reaches NMI $0.927 \pm

categories, and recovers all six DarkBench patterns, naming them after
the gold categories (anthropomorphization, brand bias, harmful

seeds. The baselines that are also asked to group by manipulation type

type leaves it at $0.749$, so its ceiling follows from the
embed-then-cluster design and not the embedder. TopicGPT with the
manipulation-type prompt reaches $0.706$ and recovers roughly six
categories, up from $0.458$ with its default topic prompt.

ten and twenty categories at NMI $0.445$ and $0.663$.

strongest baseline. The margin is smaller than on 20~Newsgroups
because the DarkBench categories carry surface-lexical signal that a
lexical method recovers in part, and it holds on a second cheap model
(gemini-3.1-flash-lite, NMI $0.884$).

\paragraph{The orchestrator occasionally enters a monotonic-add loop.}
On one 20~Newsgroups seed the orchestrator was tripped by a
monotonic-add loop, emitting eight revise calls all \textsc{add} and
growing the taxonomy from $10$ to $21$ categories without converging.
\texttt{trace.jsonl} surfaces the failure (no
\textsc{merge}/\textsc{drop}/\textsc{edit}) within seconds; we added an
auto-finalize fallback that labels the corpus against the on-disk
taxonomy, and a re-run converged.
