\paragraph{Run configuration.}
\textsc{TaxonomyAgent} uses DeepSeek-v4-Flash for both the orchestrator
and the judge on both benchmarks; the \mbox{\textsc{Single-Shot}} and
\mbox{\textsc{Iterative-Proposal}} baselines also use DeepSeek-v4-Flash, and
BERTopic uses no LLM. For 20~Newsgroups we report mean $\pm$ standard
deviation over five independently sampled random seeds on a
487-document stratified subsample (25 per gold class, minus 13 that
become empty once headers, footers, and quotes are removed, as is
standard for 20~Newsgroups). All four methods see the same documents
under the same seed. BERTopic appears at its library default \texttt{min\_topic\_size=10}
in the main table; a sweep over $\{2,5,10,15,25\}$ is summarised below
and in the released sweep data.

\paragraph{\textsc{TaxonomyAgent} leads supervised metrics.}
On the three supervised metrics \textsc{TaxonomyAgent} leads every LLM
and clustering baseline (Table~\ref{tab:20ng}): a $1.22\times$ NMI lead
over the strongest comparator, \textsc{TopicGPT}, and $2.1\times$ over
\mbox{\textsc{Single-Shot}}. \mbox{\textsc{Iterative-Proposal}} loses to
\mbox{\textsc{Single-Shot}} on every supervised metric, suggesting iterative
label refinement without a separate judge does not help on this corpus.
The concurrent \citet{brady2025iterativetax} pipeline produces a
degenerate three-cluster solution (NMI $0.164 \pm 0.092$, $58\%$ HDBSCAN
noise), the same small-cluster shape as BERTopic at library defaults; a
\texttt{min\_cluster\_size} sweep does not raise mean NMI.

\paragraph{Coherence trades off against supervised quality.}
Coherence is not uniformly in \textsc{TaxonomyAgent}'s favour (full
intrinsic metrics in the released data): \mbox{\textsc{Iterative-Proposal}}
wins NPMI with 16 lexically focused categories, and LDA and
\mbox{\textsc{Embed-Cluster-Label}} lead $C_v$ by keeping $K{=}16$ fixed. A
\texttt{min\_topic\_size} sweep that emits roughly 60 micro-clusters
closes the purity gap but sits at ARI $0.127$ against our $0.489$, a
$3.85\times$ lead.

\paragraph{Absolute spend stays under \$0.20 per 20NG run.}
A \textsc{TaxonomyAgent} run on 20NG costs $\$0.17$ on average ($8\times$
\mbox{\textsc{Embed-Cluster-Label}} at the same labeller) in $713\,$s of wall
time, still under $\$0.20$ on the 487-document corpus.

\paragraph{\textsc{TaxonomyAgent} leads every baseline on DarkBench.}
\textsc{TaxonomyAgent} reaches NMI $0.927$ and ARI $0.930$ with no hint
about the number of categories, recovering all six DarkBench patterns
under near-gold names on two of three seeds. Baselines asked to group by
manipulation type cluster at NMI $0.66$--$0.75$: BERTopic reaches

$0.706$, up from $0.458$ with its default topic prompt.

smaller than on 20~Newsgroups because DarkBench categories carry
surface-lexical signal a lexical method partly recovers, and it holds on
a second cheap model (gemini-3.1-flash-lite, NMI $0.884$).

\paragraph{The orchestrator occasionally enters a monotonic-add loop.}
On one 20~Newsgroups seed the orchestrator was tripped by a
monotonic-add loop, emitting eight revise calls all \textsc{add} and
growing the taxonomy from $10$ to $21$ categories without converging.
\texttt{trace.jsonl} surfaces the failure (no
\textsc{merge}/\textsc{drop}/\textsc{edit}) within seconds; we added an
auto-finalize fallback that labels the corpus against the on-disk
taxonomy, and a re-run converged. The fallback is applied uniformly to
all five 20NG seeds.
