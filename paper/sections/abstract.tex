We present \textsc{taxonomy\_agent}, an open-source demo that
discovers and refines a labelled taxonomy over an unlabelled text
corpus through a structural commitment: an orchestrator LLM proposes
edits through a typed dispatcher with validate-before-mutate and
per-operation failure isolation, while a stateless judge LLM
labels each item against the current taxonomy from item text alone,
with no access to the orchestrator's tool-call history or prior
label assignments. The judge runs on the cheap model and is called
once per item, so per-item cost stays low at corpus scale and the
orchestrator cannot force a label past the judge.
On a 487-document 20~Newsgroups subsample with five seeds at the
install default (DeepSeek-v4-Flash for both orchestrator and judge),
\textsc{taxonomy\_agent} reaches NMI $0.740 \pm 0.055$ and ARI
$0.489 \pm 0.085$ at $\$0.17 \pm 0.03$ per run, beating the
strongest LLM baseline (the published TopicGPT~\citep{pham2024topicgpt}
code routed through OpenRouter) by $1.22\times$ on NMI and
$1.65\times$ on ARI, and a single-shot LLM baseline by $2.1\times$ on NMI. On a
149-item synthetic corpus of failure patterns in chain-of-thought
reasoning, the system reaches NMI $0.944 \pm 0.081$ across three
seeds at $\$0.08$ per run, labelling on average $142$ of $149$
items at $100\%$ accuracy. A Claude Sonnet 4.6 orchestrator pair at
eleven times the cost reaches NMI $0.656 \pm 0.012$ on 20NG and
does not improve over the cheap pair, so orchestrator quality is
not the bottleneck at this scale. We caution that supervised metrics alone cannot separate
semantic discovery from sharper surface extraction. Code,
reproduction scripts, and corpora are at \repourl.
