We present \textsc{taxonomy\_agent}, an open-source system that
discovers and refines a labelled taxonomy over an unlabelled text
corpus through a two-tier orchestrator--judge agent. A capable
orchestrator LLM proposes edits in a small typed revise vocabulary
(\textit{add, rename, edit, merge, split, drop}), each validated
before mutation with per-op failure isolation; a cheap, parallel
judge LLM scores items and produces a misfit signal that drives
termination. The system prefers the inference provider's reported
cost over static price tables, streams partial progress to disk so
long runs are interruptible, and ships with an interactive UI for
live inspection of cost, taxonomy state, and per-item
classifications. On a 487-document 20~Newsgroups subsample with three
independently sampled random seeds, \textsc{taxonomy\_agent}
outperforms a one-shot LLM baseline and a minimal iterative-proposal
LLM baseline by $1.9\times$ on NMI and $3.3\times$ on ARI, and
retains a $2.6\times$ ARI lead over BERTopic even under an aggressive
sweep of the minimum cluster size that closes most of the purity gap by
splitting BERTopic into 60 micro-clusters. We do not run the
released TopicGPT package and do not claim a comparison against it.
On a 149-item synthetic corpus of chain-of-thought failure patterns
drawn from the 2024--2026 alignment literature, the system labels
109 of 149 items with 95.4\%\ accuracy and reaches 0.698 purity over
the full corpus, sitting $-3$ points against a supervised ceiling of
0.730 from a 14-feature surface classifier and $+20$ points above an
unsupervised k-means baseline on the same features. We caution that
the metrics alone cannot cleanly attribute the margin between
semantic discovery and sharper surface extraction. Code,
reproduction scripts, and the corpora are released at
\url{https://github.com/ngqm/taxonomy-agent}.
