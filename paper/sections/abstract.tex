We present \textsc{taxonomy\_agent}, an open-source demo that
discovers and refines a labelled taxonomy over an unlabelled text
corpus and ships with the operational tooling a stranger needs to
actually use it: live native provider cost accounting split by
orchestrator and judge, partial-progress streaming to disk so a
killed run leaves recoverable state, vendor-agnostic routing across
any chat-completions provider, and a Streamlit Inspect tab that
replays the trace, partial taxonomies, and cost split of any run.
Beneath sits a two-tier agent: an orchestrator LLM proposes edits
through a typed revise dispatcher over six verbs
(\textit{add, rename, edit, merge, split, drop}), each validated
before mutation with per-operation failure isolation, while a
parallel stateless judge re-derives labels from each item and
reports a don't-fit rate the orchestrator reads as a termination
signal.
On a 487-document 20~Newsgroups subsample with four seeds at the
install default (DeepSeek-v4-Flash for both orchestrator and judge),
\textsc{taxonomy\_agent} reaches NMI $0.727 \pm 0.055$ and ARI
$0.470 \pm 0.087$ at USD $0.17 \pm 0.04$ per run, beating a
model-matched embed-cluster-LLM reimplementation of
TnT-LLM~\citep{wan2024tntllm} by $1.5\times$ on NMI and $1.9\times$
on ARI and a single-shot LLM baseline by $2.1\times$ on NMI. On a
149-item synthetic corpus of failure patterns in chain-of-thought
reasoning, the system reaches NMI $0.944 \pm 0.081$ across three
seeds at USD $0.08$ per run, labelling on average $142$ of $149$
items at $100\%$ accuracy and beating every LLM and clustering
discovery baseline by more than $0.7$ NMI. The publication and
install configurations are the same; a Claude Sonnet 4.6
orchestrator pair costs roughly eleven times more without a
statistically distinguishable NMI gain on 20NG. We caution
that supervised metrics alone cannot separate semantic discovery
from sharper surface extraction. Code, reproduction scripts, and
the corpora are at \repourl.
