We present \textsc{TaxonomyAgent}, an open-source Python package and
Streamlit interface for discovering structured taxonomies in unstructured
text. Given a corpus and a one-sentence goal instruction,
\textsc{TaxonomyAgent} discovers a labelled taxonomy and assigns every
item to a named category; a single command launches the run, and the UI
streams the agent's trace, the evolving taxonomy, and partial state as
items are labelled. The goal-instruction mechanism makes discovery
steerable toward a chosen axis of variation, which lets us target an
underserved task in LLM alignment research: surfacing failure modes
and reasoning strategies in chain-of-thought traces. On a 149-item
synthetic corpus of CoT failure patterns, \textsc{TaxonomyAgent}
reaches NMI 0.944 and labels 95\% of items at perfect accuracy, while
the published TopicGPT pipeline collapses to a single surface topic.
On 20~Newsgroups, \textsc{TaxonomyAgent} also handles classical
topic modeling, reaching NMI 0.740 and beating TopicGPT by
$1.22\times$. Internally, two LLM roles are separated by a structured
contract: an orchestrator proposes typed taxonomy edits while a
stateless judge labels each item from item text alone, keeping
per-item inference cost linear in corpus size. Code, reproduction
scripts, and corpora are at \repourl.
