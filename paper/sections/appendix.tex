\section*{A\quad Why a Single-Source Synthetic CoT Corpus}
\label{app:synthetic-corpus}

Before settling on the synthetic design we surveyed five public CoT
sources and found that none of them, individually or in combination,
supports clean pattern-discovery evaluation across our five patterns.
The Sharma et al.\ sycophancy
stimuli~\citep{sharma2023sycophancy} are user-side prompts and do not
contain annotated CoT pivots. The Perez et al.\ model-written
evaluations~\citep{perez2022discovering} provide only statistical
proxies with no per-trace structural labels.
FaithCoT-Bench~\citep{shen2026faithcot} conflates arithmetic errors
with hallucinations and does not separate post-hoc rationalisation
from unfaithful paraphrase. The Denison
sycophancy-to-subterfuge transcripts~\citep{denison2024subterfuge}
contain roughly seven explicit examples of verbalised reward hacking.
PRM800K~\citep{lightman2023prm800k} labels per-step correctness
without naming the pattern. No public dataset provides annotated CoT
pivots for more than one of our five patterns, and combining datasets
to cover all five makes source detection a near-perfect proxy for
pattern detection (the hybrid corpus reaches 95\%\ surface-feature
accuracy versus 73\%\ on the single-source corpus, making the hybrid
unevaluable as a benchmark). Construct-validity arguments for
synthetic single-source CoT evaluation in this regime follow the
precedents established by~\citet{lanham2023faithfulness}
and~\citet{turpin2023biased}.

The 0.730 surface ceiling is a supervised logistic regression on 14
surface features trained under 5-fold stratified cross-validation on
the same 149 items the discovery systems see; it is a supervised
oracle ceiling for what surface shape alone can do. Taxonomy\_agent's
purity is the optimal-mapping purity of an unsupervised discovered
partition; the two quantities are not strictly commensurable, which is
why the body reports the CoT result two ways: accuracy on the 132
non-\texttt{other} items at the install default, directly comparable
to $0.730$, and coverage-adjusted purity over all 149 items treating
\texttt{other} as a sixth class.

\section*{B\quad Per-Sub-Domain Stability on the CoT Corpus}
\label{app:per-domain}

We tagged each item with a problem-domain heuristic from the problem
text and recomputed precision-at-coverage, coverage, purity, and NMI
per sub-domain (Table~\ref{tab:cot_per_domain}). Precision on
labelled items is $100\%$ across every sub-domain with at least five
items; coverage varies between 0.80 and 1.00; NMI stays in the
$0.82$--$1.00$ band. The number-theory slice ($n{=}5$) is too thin
to support a stability claim alone; the cross-sub-domain reading
rests on algebra (50), geometry (40), the residual catch-all (39),
and combinatorics (15).

\section*{C\quad Convergence Signal Cross-Check}
\label{app:convergence}

Across the 12 classify events from the original three-seed 20NG
taxonomy\_agent runs at the Sonnet pair (the cross-check predates
the install default switch and the install default produces analogous
saturation), the don't-fit trajectories are
$[0.00,0.00,0.00,0.00]$,
$[0.05,0.00,0.00,0.00]$, and
$[0.10,0.05,0.00,0.05]$:
the signal is bounded between 0.00 and 0.10, descends quickly toward
the floor in two of three seeds, and shows a non-monotone
$0.00 \to 0.05$ bounce at the end of the third seed. Across the
three terminal $(\text{don't-fit}, \text{purity})$ pairs the Spearman
correlation is $\rho = -0.87$ ($p = 0.33$ at $n = 3$), with the same
sign for NMI and opposite sign for NPMI. The signal saturates at the
floor too quickly to function as a quantitative leading indicator,
so we use it as an operational stopping default in \S\ref{sec:system}
and report external metrics separately.

\section*{D\quad Typed-Dispatcher Ablation Detail}
\label{app:ablation}

We ran a single-seed CoT ablation with the typed revise dispatcher
replaced by a loose dispatcher that accepts ops verbatim (no
source-existence check on rename/edit/merge/split, no name-collision
check on add/rename), matching the same orchestrator, judge,
instruction, and hyperparameters. The typed CoT run produces eight
trace events including one successful \textsc{revise} call, finishes
in 197\,s, and costs $\$2.23$ total. The loose run we terminated at
$\$1.35$ of spend (six orchestrator calls in 90\,s) contained two
\textsc{novelties} calls (each returning zero candidates), two
\textsc{classify} probes (don't-fit rates 0.11 and 0.13), and zero
\textsc{revise} calls, leaving the working taxonomy empty at
termination. Two interpretations are consistent with this single
observation: the typed validation log gives the orchestrator a
feedback signal it uses, or ordinary stochasticity produced the
outcome (the judge's two novelties calls happened to return empty
candidate sets in the loose run). A
single seed cannot distinguish these.

\section*{F\quad Configuration and Reproducibility}
\label{app:repro}

All numbers in the body and the preceding appendix sections were
produced from a single commit (\texttt{0910dc7}); the repository at
\repourl{} pins prompts, hyperparameters, and seeds. The OpenRouter
slugs in the publication runs are
\texttt{deepseek/deepseek-v4-flash},
\texttt{anthropic/claude-sonnet-4.6},
\texttt{anthropic/claude-opus-4.8} (CoT-Pattern verifier only),
\texttt{google/gemini-3.1-flash-lite} (CoT-Pattern generation only),
and \texttt{meta-llama/llama-3.2-3b-instruct} (Brady-Islam
reimplementation); all calls were routed during 2026-04 through
2026-06. Temperature is the OpenRouter default for each model and
top-$p$ is unconstrained. The orchestrator is capped at
$\texttt{max\_iterations}{=}80$ ReAct steps and a
$\texttt{pool\_limit}$ of $50$ for the judge.

Baselines: \texttt{embed\_cluster\_llm} embeds with
\texttt{sentence-transformers/all-MiniLM-L6-v2}~\citep{reimers2019sbert} (v3.2.1), runs
sklearn~\citep{pedregosa2011sklearn} k-means at $K{=}16$ with \texttt{random\_state=42},
and names each cluster with one DeepSeek-v4-Flash call sampled from
five items. \texttt{lda}~\citep{blei2003lda} uses sklearn
\texttt{LatentDirichletAllocation} at
$K{=}16$, \texttt{learning\_method='batch'}, \texttt{max\_iter=50},
\texttt{random\_state=42}, on a $1$-$2$-gram \texttt{CountVectorizer}.
BERTopic v0.16 with library-default UMAP~\citep{mcinnes2018umap}
($n\_neighbors{=}15$, $n\_components{=}5$, $min\_dist{=}0.0$,
\texttt{random\_state=42}) and HDBSCAN~\citep{mcinnes2017hdbscan}
($min\_cluster\_size{=}10$, $min\_samples=$None,
\texttt{euclidean}, \texttt{eom}). Brady-Islam reuses the authors'
embedding (\texttt{all-MiniLM-L6-v2}) and HDBSCAN configuration at
\texttt{min\_cluster\_size=5}; a sweep over $\{5,10,15,25,50\}$ did
not raise mean NMI above $0.20$. The 14 surface features for
LR@surface (\S\ref{sec:eval-results-cot}) and the CoT-Pattern
generation and verifier prompts are at
\texttt{paper/data/cot\_features.py} and \texttt{paper/data/cot\_gen/}
in the same commit.

Metric definitions: NMI is sklearn's
\texttt{normalized\_mutual\_info\_score} with arithmetic averaging;
ARI is \texttt{adjusted\_rand\_score}; purity is computed by the
Hungarian-optimal one-to-one mapping between discovered and gold
clusters with non-mapped items contributing zero; NPMI uses a
sliding window of $10$ tokens via gensim's \texttt{CoherenceModel};
$C_v$ uses the same window; label redundancy is the fraction of
pairs of discovered categories with cosine similarity above $0.85$
on \texttt{all-MiniLM-L6-v2} embeddings of the label name and
description concatenated.
