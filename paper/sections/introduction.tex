\section{Introduction}
\label{sec:intro}

Constructing a labelled taxonomy over an unlabelled corpus is a recurring
bottleneck in applied NLP. Annotation guidelines for supervised
classification, codebooks for qualitative research, summarisation schemas
for analyst-facing dashboards, and policy hierarchies for content
moderation all begin life as a manual taxonomy. Producing one typically
requires domain experts to read hundreds of documents, draft candidate
categories, argue over overlaps, and iterate until coverage and
disjointness are acceptable. The process is slow, expensive, and difficult
to reproduce, and the resulting taxonomy is often a brittle artefact that
nobody is incentivised to revisit when the underlying corpus drifts.

Recent work has shown that large language models (LLMs) can replace much
of this manual labour. TopicGPT~\citep{pham2024topicgpt} prompts an LLM
to generate topic names and definitions from sampled documents with a
refinement pass, while TnT-LLM~\citep{wan2024tntllm} stages a two-phase
pipeline that summarises a corpus into candidate labels and then distils
a lightweight classifier. Co-DETECT~\citep{codetect2025} is last year's
EMNLP System Demonstration on collaborative codebook construction with
LLMs and the closest peer to our submission; we sit at an orthogonal
operating point (taxonomy discovery from scratch over an unlabelled
corpus, without a seed sketch and without a human in the loop) and
discuss the relationship in \S\ref{sec:related}. Against this backdrop,
our contribution is a careful engineering recomposition: we narrow the
orchestrator's edit surface to a typed validated DSL with per-operation
failure isolation, decouple labelling into a parallel judge, and ship
the result with operational tooling for live cost accounting,
partial-progress saving, and a web UI.

Empirically, at the install default (DeepSeek-v4-Flash for both
orchestrator and judge), the system reaches NMI $0.727 \pm 0.055$
and ARI $0.470 \pm 0.087$ on 20~Newsgroups at USD $0.17$ per run,
beating a one-shot LLM baseline by $2.1\times$ on NMI and
$4.7\times$ on ARI and a model-matched embed-cluster-LLM
reimplementation of TnT-LLM by $1.5\times$ on NMI and $1.9\times$
on ARI (\S\ref{sec:eval-results-20ng}). A Sonnet 4.6 orchestrator
pair at eleven times the cost lands at NMI $0.656 \pm 0.012$,
inside the cheap pair's interval, so we make the cheap pair the
publication and install default. On the synthetic CoT-Pattern
corpus the system reaches NMI $0.944 \pm 0.081$ across three seeds
at USD $0.08$ per run, labelling on average $142$ of $149$ items at
$100\%$ accuracy (\S\ref{sec:eval-results-cot}).

\noindent Our contributions are:
\begin{itemize}
  \item \textbf{Operational tooling for a tool a stranger can use}
        (Appendix~A): live native provider cost accounting
        broken down by orchestrator and judge, partial-progress
        streaming so a killed run leaves recoverable state,
        vendor-agnostic routing, and a web UI surfacing all of the
        above. We claim this layer as the strongest demonstration
        contribution.
  \item \textbf{A typed dispatcher that validates before mutating} over
        six standard verbs (\textit{add, rename, edit, merge, split,
        drop}) with per-operation failure isolation, providing an
        auditable \texttt{trace.jsonl} of every edit.
  \item \textbf{A parallel stateless judge architecture}, building on
        the generator and labeller split of
        TnT-LLM~\citep{wan2024tntllm}, with a don't-fit rate that the
        orchestrator treats as a termination signal.
  \item \textbf{An open-source artefact at} \repourl{} with a
        \texttt{pip install} path, reproducible 20NG and CoT benchmark
        configurations, and a Streamlit Inspect tab that replays the
        trace, partial taxonomies, and cost split of any run.
\end{itemize}
