\section*{Limitations}
\label{sec:limitations}

\paragraph{Surface vs.\ semantic on CoT.} Our $100\%$ precision on
the 132 labelled items at the install default lies within the range
a sharper surface extractor could plausibly produce against the
supervised 0.730 ceiling. The supervised metrics alone cannot
distinguish modest semantic discovery on top of structural pattern
shape from sharper extraction of the same shape. The earlier
differential under-recovery of \texttt{hallucinated\_premise} we
read as evidence for the second reading was specific to the Sonnet
orchestrator pair; the cheap pair recovers it at 23 of 29, so the
extractive interpretation is no longer the simplest fit. We do not
claim the CoT result settles the question, but the cross-pair
robustness leans against the most pessimistic read.

\paragraph{Self-reported convergence is circular and saturates.} The
don't-fit rate is computed by the same judge that produced the
labels; a stable terminal rate just says the judge is self-consistent
with respect to its own labelling function. Empirically the signal
saturates at the floor (9 of 12 classify events report
$\leq 0.05$), and the threshold and floor were calibrated on the
benchmarks we evaluate on, so convergence should be read as fitted
to our test corpora.

\paragraph{Synthetic CoT corpus and 20NG contamination.} The 149-item
CoT benchmark covers competition mathematics only, each trace
generated by an LLM under a pattern injector and verified by Claude
Opus. The orchestrator is Claude Sonnet, a different size in the
same family, so gold labels and orchestrator may share heuristics.
20~Newsgroups is almost certainly in pretraining of both
DeepSeek and Claude, so the $0.713$ NMI at the install default may
partly reflect memorisation. A corpus with multiple sources per
pattern and explicit deconfounding is the natural next step.

\paragraph{Partial component ablations and missing baselines.} The
single-seed loose-dispatcher ablation on CoT cannot distinguish a
missing-feedback story from stochasticity; the orchestrator/judge
split, the don't-fit criterion, and the parallel judge dispatcher
remain unablated. Co-DETECT, TaxoAdapt, GoalEx, and the full
TnT-LLM distillation pipeline are not run as direct baselines; we
flag these gaps.
