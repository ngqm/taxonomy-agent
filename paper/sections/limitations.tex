\section*{Limitations}
\label{sec:limitations}

Our convergence signal is self-reported: the judge LLM is, in effect,
marking its own homework when it reports the don't-fit rate that
terminates the loop. We mitigate this with two design choices and an
external check. The design choices are a minimum-iteration floor that
prevents premature termination on small early samples, and the
orchestrator--judge model split that makes the judge structurally
independent of the model proposing edits. The external check is the
scatter plot of terminal don't-fit rate against NPMI and external
purity in Section~\ref{sec:eval}, which tests whether the self-reported
signal tracks ground-truth quality.

Cost grows with iteration count. Each iteration spends one orchestrator
turn and one judge fan-out, and pathological corpora may need many
iterations to stabilise. The Streamlit UI exposes running cost so the
operator can stop early, but we do not currently impose a hard budget.

All experiments in this paper are English-only and use a single
realisation of the 20~Newsgroups corpus subset. We do not yet have
evidence that the typed revise vocabulary generalises across languages,
nor that the convergence behaviour is stable across very different
corpus sizes or genres (long-form scientific text, code, multi-turn
dialogue). \todo{Note any additional limitations surfaced by the eval.}
