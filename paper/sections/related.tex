\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\paragraph{LLM topic discovery and taxonomy induction.}
TopicGPT~\citep{pham2024topicgpt} prompts an LLM to generate
topic labels from sampled documents with a refinement pass.
TnT-LLM~\citep{wan2024tntllm} stages a summarise-then-distil
pipeline that anticipates our generator-and-labeller split, though
its refine step is a single batched summarisation; our
\texttt{embed\_cluster\_llm} baseline reimplements this shape.
BERTopic~\citep{grootendorst2022bertopic} remains the strongest
non-LLM baseline. Three concurrent 2025 systems sit closest to ours:
\citet{brady2025iterativetax} iteratively prompt an LLM to reuse or
generate a label per embedding cluster;
LOGOS~\citep{pi2025logos} runs a grounded-theory pipeline with
LLM coding, semantic clustering, and iterative codebook refinement,
reporting 88.2\% expert-schema match; and
\citet{zhu2025contextawaretax} build LLM-guided hierarchical
taxonomies of scientific papers. \textsc{taxonomy\_agent} differs on
three operating axes: a typed edit DSL with per-operation failure
isolation, an online ReAct loop rather than offline batched refine,
and first-class cost and partial-progress accounting through a
walk-up Streamlit surface.
\citet{yang2025llmitl} put an LLM inside a neural topic-model
training loop, and \citet{huang2025clustasclass} reframe clustering
as direct classification through an LLM (close to our `single\_shot'
baseline). Adjacent threads on goal-driven
clustering~\citep{wang2023goalex}, concept
induction~\citep{lam2024lloom}, taxonomy
adaptation~\citep{taxoadapt2025}, and LLM-guided semantic
clustering~\citep{llmguided2025} target different input contracts;
\citet{tan2024llmannot} survey the broader landscape.

\paragraph{Multi-agent annotation and codebook construction.}
Co-DETECT~\citep{codetect2025} is last year's EMNLP demonstration on
collaborative codebook construction from a sketch codebook and a
human in the loop. Recent multi-agent systems decompose the
proposer-verifier roles further: Thematic-LM~\citep{deng2025thematiclm}
runs coder, aggregator, and reviewer agents in parallel;
TAMA~\citep{kim2025tama} adds expert oversight on clinical
interviews; and \citet{liu2025aiannotorch} evaluate LLM verifiers as
a quality layer on LLM annotators. \textsc{taxonomy\_agent} sits at
an orthogonal operating point: we discover a candidate taxonomy from
scratch over an unlabelled corpus with a single goal instruction, no
seed sketch and no human in the loop, then hand off the trace,
working taxonomy, and per-item classifications for downstream review
or Co-DETECT-style refinement. The orchestrator-and-judge separation
reduces the multi-agent coordination surface to a single ReAct loop.

\paragraph{Chain-of-thought analysis.}
The CoT-Pattern corpus borrows its five failure modes from the
CoT-faithfulness literature~\citep{lanham2023faithfulness,
turpin2023biased}; the hint taxonomy in
\citet{chen2025reasoningmodels} maps almost one-to-one onto our
labels. FaithCoT-Bench~\citep{xu2025faithcotbench} and
PRMBench~\citep{song2025prmbench} ship instance-level and
step-level CoT-failure benchmarks. \citet{lee2025stepwiseeval}
survey step-level reasoning evaluation.
\textsc{taxonomy\_agent} does not propose a new CoT analysis method;
we use CoT-Pattern as a second-domain stress test of the discovery
loop, motivated by the position paper of
\citet{korbak2025cotmonitor}.

\paragraph{Positioning.}
Iteration, proposer-and-labeller separation, and the
add/rename/merge/split/drop verbs are all standard. The
contribution is two structural commitments: a typed dispatcher with
validate-before-mutate and per-operation failure isolation, and a
stateless judge that re-derives each item's category from the item
text alone. The first ensures malformed edits never corrupt state;
the second makes cost linear in the corpus on the cheap model and
prevents the orchestrator from forcing a label past the judge.
