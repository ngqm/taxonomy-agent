\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\paragraph{LLM topic discovery and classical baselines.}
TopicGPT~\citep{pham2024topicgpt} prompts an LLM to generate
human-readable topic labels from sampled documents and assigns the
remaining corpus to those labels, with a refinement pass that merges
and prunes; refinement is bundled into the prompt and the system is
not released as an interactive tool with first-class cost or
partial-progress accounting. TnT-LLM~\citep{wan2024tntllm} stages a
two-phase pipeline that first summarises batches into candidate
labels and then distils a lightweight classifier for full-corpus
labelling, anticipating our orchestrator/judge split, though its
``refine'' step is a single batched summarisation inside an offline
production pipeline. Our \texttt{embed\_cluster\_llm} baseline in
Table~\ref{tab:20ng} is a minimal reimplementation of this
generator-then-labeller shape for direct comparison.
BERTopic~\citep{grootendorst2022bertopic} is the strongest non-LLM
baseline: embed, UMAP, HDBSCAN, c-TF-IDF; fast and reproducible,
though its clusters are bags of keywords with no human-readable
category definitions and the taxonomy cannot be edited in a typed
way once produced. Other recent threads on goal-driven
clustering~\citep{wang2023goalex}, concept
induction~\citep{lam2024lloom}, taxonomy
adaptation~\citep{taxoadapt2025}, LLM-guided semantic
clustering~\citep{llmguided2025}, and the broader LLMs-for-annotation
landscape~\citep{tan2024llmannot} sit adjacent but target different
input contracts or output shapes.

\paragraph{Co-DETECT.}
Co-DETECT~\citep{codetect2025} is last year's EMNLP System
Demonstration on collaborative codebook construction with LLMs: a
domain expert supplies a sketch codebook and a small dataset, the
LLM annotates and surfaces edge cases the sketch fails to cover, and
the human accepts, rejects, or amends each rule before the codebook
is redeployed. The artefact targets the ongoing refinement loop a
policy team would sit inside for weeks.
\textsc{taxonomy\_agent} sits at an orthogonal operating point: we
discover a candidate taxonomy from scratch over an unlabelled corpus
without a seed sketch and without a human in the loop, then hand off
the trace, the working taxonomy, and the per-item classifications
for downstream human review or for Co-DETECT-style refinement. We do
not run a direct comparison because the input contracts differ
(seed codebook plus edge cases versus unlabelled corpus plus goal
instruction) and no publicly released Co-DETECT artefact ran on
either of our benchmarks at submission time.

\paragraph{Positioning.}
We do not claim a wholly new mechanism: TopicGPT iterates, TnT-LLM
separates a generator from a labeller, ReAct-style
agents~\citep{yao2022react} structure tool-using LLMs, and the
add/rename/merge/split/drop verbs are the standard taxonomy edit
vocabulary. The contribution is an operational recomposition that
prior LLM topic-discovery and codebook systems treat as out of
scope: first-class cost and partial-progress accounting, a typed
dispatcher with per-operation failure isolation, and a parallel
stateless judge with an \texttt{other} escape and a saturated
don't-fit termination signal.
