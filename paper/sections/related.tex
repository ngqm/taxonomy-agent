\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\paragraph{Chain-of-thought analysis and LLM-output evaluation.}
A growing body of work analyses chain-of-thought (CoT) reasoning
traces for safety-relevant patterns.
FaithCoT-Bench~\citep{shen2026faithcot} and
PRMBench~\citep{song2025prmbench} provide instance- and step-level
benchmarks of CoT faithfulness against known failure categories. The
hint taxonomy in~\citet{chen2025reasoningmodels} catalogues
hint-utilisation patterns in reasoning models, and
\citet{korbak2025cotmonitor} discuss CoT monitorability as a safety
opportunity. Earlier work establishes that LLM CoT is often
unfaithful~\citep{turpin2023biased, lanham2023faithfulness} and that
LLMs exhibit
sycophancy~\citep{sharma2023sycophancy} and reward-tampering
behaviour~\citep{denison2024subterfuge, perez2022discovering}.
\citet{lee2025stepwiseeval} survey the broader literature on
stepwise reasoning evaluation. These benchmarks evaluate models
against categories fixed in advance and assume the category set is
given.

\paragraph{Analysing populations of reasoning traces.}
A separate line studies not a single chain of thought but a whole
collection of them, and every method we are aware of either imposes a
fixed category set or builds a bespoke one for each prompt. Thought
Anchors~\citep{bogdan2025thoughtanchors} classifies each sentence of a
trace into eight predefined step types (problem setup, plan
generation, fact retrieval, active computation, uncertainty
management, result consolidation, self-checking, and final-answer
emission), then measures which sentences drive the answer through
counterfactual resampling and attention analysis.
\citet{li2026schoenfeld} map model reasoning onto a four-part taxonomy
imported from Schoenfeld's theory of human mathematical
problem-solving (knowledge, strategies, monitoring, and beliefs).
\citet{li2026performative} study step-level faithfulness and argue
that many reasoning steps are formatted for an evaluator instead of
driving the computation, so a chain of thought is an incomplete
oversight signal. Closest to our setting,
\citet{tyagi2026globalcot} cluster around a thousand traces per prompt
into strategy graphs, but their algorithmic clustering has a language
model write a keyword dictionary for each new prompt that a substring
matcher then applies. \textsc{TaxonomyAgent} instead induces the
category set from an unlabelled corpus and one goal instruction, so
the analyst chooses the axis of variation without importing a fixed
schema or writing a per-prompt keyword dictionary.

\paragraph{Multi-agent annotation and codebook construction.}
Co-DETECT~\citep{codetect2025} is last year's EMNLP demonstration on
collaborative codebook construction from a sketch codebook and a
human in the loop. Recent multi-agent systems decompose the
proposer-verifier roles further:
Thematic-LM~\citep{qiao2025thematiclm} runs coder, aggregator, and
reviewer agents in parallel; TAMA~\citep{xu2025tama} adds expert
oversight on clinical interviews; and \citet{liu2025aiannotorch}
evaluate LLM verifiers as a quality layer on LLM annotators.
\textsc{TaxonomyAgent} sits at an orthogonal operating point: we
discover a candidate taxonomy from scratch over an unlabelled corpus
with a single goal instruction, no seed sketch and no human in the
loop, then hand off the trace, working taxonomy, and per-item
classifications for downstream review or Co-DETECT-style refinement.
The orchestrator-and-judge separation reduces multi-agent
coordination to a single ReAct loop.

\paragraph{LLM topic discovery and taxonomy induction.}
TopicGPT~\citep{pham2024topicgpt} prompts an LLM to generate topic
labels from sampled documents with a refinement pass.
TnT-LLM~\citep{wan2024tntllm} stages a summarise-then-distil
pipeline that anticipates our generator-and-labeller split, though
its refine step is a single batched summarisation.
BERTopic~\citep{grootendorst2022bertopic} remains the strongest
non-LLM baseline. Three concurrent 2025 systems sit closest to ours
on the topic-modeling axis: \citet{brady2025iterativetax} iteratively
prompt an LLM to reuse or generate a label per embedding cluster;
LOGOS~\citep{pi2025logos} runs a grounded-theory pipeline with LLM
coding, semantic clustering, and iterative codebook refinement; and
\citet{zhu2025contextawaretax} build LLM-guided hierarchical
taxonomies of scientific papers. \citet{yang2025llmitl} put an LLM
inside a neural topic-model training loop, and
\citet{huang2025clustasclass} reframe clustering as direct
classification through an LLM (close to our \textsc{Single-Shot}
baseline). Adjacent threads on goal-driven
clustering~\citep{wang2023goalex}, concept
induction~\citep{lam2024lloom}, taxonomy
adaptation~\citep{taxoadapt2025}, and LLM-guided semantic
clustering~\citep{llmguided2025} target different input contracts;
\citet{tan2024llmannot} survey the broader landscape. None of these
systems expose a goal-instruction mechanism that allows the analyst
to direct discovery toward a non-topical axis of variation, which is
the gap \textsc{TaxonomyAgent} addresses.
