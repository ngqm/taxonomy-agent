\section{Related Work}
\label{sec:related}

\paragraph{LLM topic discovery and taxonomy induction.}
TopicGPT~\citep{pham2024topicgpt} prompts an LLM to generate
human-readable topic labels from sampled documents and assigns the
remaining corpus to those labels, with a refinement pass merged into
the prompt. TnT-LLM~\citep{wan2024tntllm} stages a two-phase pipeline
that summarises batches into candidate labels and then distils a
lightweight classifier for full-corpus labelling, anticipating the
generator and labeller split, though its refine step is a single
batched summarisation inside an offline production pipeline. Our
\texttt{embed\_cluster\_llm} baseline in Table~\ref{tab:20ng} is a
minimal reimplementation of this generator-then-labeller shape.
BERTopic~\citep{grootendorst2022bertopic} remains the strongest
non-LLM baseline. Three concurrent 2025 systems sit closest to ours.
\citet{brady2025iterativetax} iteratively prompt an LLM to either
reuse an existing label or generate a new one per embedding cluster;
LOGOS~\citep{pi2025logos} runs a full grounded-theory pipeline with
LLM coding, semantic clustering, and iterative codebook refinement,
reporting 88.2\% expert-schema match; and
\citet{zhu2025contextawaretax} build LLM-guided hierarchical
taxonomies of scientific papers. \textsc{taxonomy\_agent} differs on
three operating axes: a typed edit DSL with per-operation failure
isolation, an online ReAct loop rather than offline batched refine,
and first-class cost and partial-progress accounting through a
walk-up Streamlit surface. \citet{yang2025llmitl}, in contrast, place
an LLM inside the training loop of a neural topic model.
\citet{huang2025clustasclass} reframe clustering as direct
classification through an LLM, which our `single\_shot' baseline
approximates. Other 2025 work on goal-driven
clustering~\citep{wang2023goalex}, concept
induction~\citep{lam2024lloom}, taxonomy
adaptation~\citep{taxoadapt2025}, and LLM-guided semantic
clustering~\citep{llmguided2025} addresses different input contracts
or output shapes; \citet{tan2024llmannot} survey the broader
LLM-annotation landscape.

\paragraph{Multi-agent annotation and codebook construction.}
Co-DETECT~\citep{codetect2025} is last year's EMNLP demonstration on
collaborative codebook construction: a domain expert supplies a
sketch codebook and the LLM surfaces edge cases the sketch fails to
cover. The artefact targets a refinement loop a policy team would
sit inside for weeks. Thematic-LM~\citep{deng2025thematiclm} runs
coder, aggregator, and reviewer agents in parallel over a corpus
with an adaptive codebook; TAMA~\citep{kim2025tama} adds expert
oversight on clinical interviews; and
\citet{liu2025aiannotorch} evaluate LLM verifiers as a quality
layer on top of LLM annotators. \textsc{taxonomy\_agent} sits at an
orthogonal operating point: we discover a candidate taxonomy from
scratch over an unlabelled corpus with a single goal instruction, no
seed sketch and no human in the loop, then hand off the trace, the
working taxonomy, and the per-item classifications for downstream
human review or Co-DETECT-style refinement. The orchestrator and
judge separate the proposer and verifier roles into one tool-using
agent and one stateless function, which reduces the multi-agent
coordination surface to a single ReAct loop with a typed dispatcher.

\paragraph{Chain-of-thought analysis.}
The CoT-Pattern corpus borrows its five injected failure modes from
the CoT-faithfulness literature~\citep{lanham2023faithfulness,
turpin2023biased}; the hint taxonomy in
\citet{chen2025reasoningmodels} maps almost one-to-one onto our
labels. FaithCoT-Bench~\citep{xu2025faithcotbench} releases an
instance-level CoT-failure benchmark with eleven detection
baselines, and PRMBench~\citep{song2025prmbench} benchmarks
process-level reward models at step granularity.
\citet{lee2025stepwiseeval} survey the step-level reasoning
evaluation literature under a four-axis taxonomy.
\textsc{taxonomy\_agent} does not propose a new CoT analysis method;
we use CoT-Pattern as a second-domain stress test of the discovery
loop, and the position paper of \citet{korbak2025cotmonitor} frames
the broader motivation for tooling that surfaces reasoning-trace
patterns.

\paragraph{Positioning.}
We do not claim a wholly new mechanism: TopicGPT iterates, TnT-LLM
and LOGOS separate proposal from labelling, ReAct-style
agents~\citep{yao2022react} structure tool-using LLMs, and the
add/rename/merge/split/drop verbs are standard taxonomy edit
vocabulary~\citep{taxoadapt2025}. The contribution is an operational
recomposition that 2025 LLM topic discovery and codebook systems
treat as out of scope: first-class cost and partial-progress
accounting, a typed dispatcher with per-operation failure isolation,
and a parallel stateless judge with an \texttt{other} escape and a
don't-fit termination signal, wrapped in a walk-up Streamlit surface.
