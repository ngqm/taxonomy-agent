\subsection{Evaluation Methodology}
\label{sec:eval-method}

We evaluate \textsc{taxonomy\_agent} on two benchmarks. \textbf{20~Newsgroups}~\citep{lang1995newsgroups} probes whether the orchestrator--judge loop matches established topic-modeling baselines on a corpus with known-good gold structure; we use a 487-document stratified subsample (25 per class, 13 emptied by sklearn's header/footer/quote filter) under four independently sampled random seeds at the install default and three at the Sonnet pair. \textbf{CoT-Pattern} is a 149-item corpus of chain-of-thought traces evaluated under three seeds at the install default and one at the Sonnet pair, injected with five safety-relevant failure modes (sycophantic capitulation, post-hoc rationalization, unfaithful paraphrase, reward-hack verbalization, hallucinated premise), drawn from MATH-500 Level~4--5 problems, generated by Gemini-3.1-Flash-Lite, and filtered by a unanimous Claude Opus 4.8 three-classifier ensemble. We compare \textsc{taxonomy\_agent} at the install default (DeepSeek-v4-Flash for both orchestrator and judge) against four baselines: BERTopic~\citep{grootendorst2022bertopic} at library defaults with a \texttt{min\_topic\_size} sweep, a single-shot LLM proposer-plus-labeller, an iterative-proposal LLM port of TopicGPT~\citep{pham2024topicgpt}, and an embed-cluster-LLM pipeline in the TnT-LLM family. We report external metrics (purity, NMI, ARI), intrinsic topic-quality metrics (NPMI, $C_v$ coherence~\citep{rehurek2010gensim}, label redundancy), dollar cost from OpenRouter \texttt{usage.cost}, and wall-clock time. Construct-validity arguments for the single-source synthetic CoT corpus, per-pattern source survey, and the commensurability discussion for purity vs.\ accuracy on CoT appear in Appendix~A.
